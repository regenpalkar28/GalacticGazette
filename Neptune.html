Neptune

Overview

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in the Solar System. It's a deep blue ice giant known for supersonic winds and dynamic weather. Despite being smaller than Uranus, Neptune is more massive and has a strong magnetic field and faint ring system. It’s named after the Roman god of the sea.

True color image of Neptune captured by Voyager 2

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: About 49,244 km
  • Mass: 1.024 × 1026 kg (17 times Earth’s mass)
  • Moons: 14 known moons; Triton is the largest and has retrograde orbit
  • Rings: 5 main rings composed of dust and ice particles
  • Atmosphere: Extremely dynamic, with winds reaching 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph)

Chemical Composition

  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane
  • Interior: Ice and rock, surrounding a rocky core

Space Expeditions

  • Voyager 2 (1989): The only spacecraft to fly by Neptune; discovered six new moons, Neptune’s Great Dark Spot, and its faint rings
  • Future Missions: Scientists propose orbiter missions to study Neptune and Triton, its largest moon, believed to be a captured Kuiper Belt object
