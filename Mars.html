Mars

Overview

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance caused by iron oxide on its surface.

It has seasons, polar ice caps, canyons, extinct volcanoes, and is home to Olympus Mons—the tallest volcano in the solar system. Mars has a thin atmosphere and experiences dust storms. It has two moons: Phobos and Deimos.

True color image of Mars

Physical Characteristics

  • Surface Temperature: -125°C to 20°C (-195°F to 70°F)
  • Surface Features: Craters, valleys, volcanoes, polar ice caps
  • Atmosphere: Thin, mostly carbon dioxide
  • Gravity: About 38% of Earth’s gravity

Chemical Composition

  • Atmosphere: 95% CO₂, 2.6% N₂, 1.9% Ar
  • Surface: Iron-rich basaltic rock and dust
  • Interior: Iron, nickel, and sulfur core; silicate mantle

Space Expeditions

  • Viking 1 & 2 (1975–1982): First successful landers on Mars.
  • Curiosity Rover (2012–Present): Studying climate and geology.

    • Curiosity rover exploring Mars

  • Perseverance Rover (2021–Present): Searching for signs of past life and collecting rock samples.

    • Perseverance rover exploring Mars

  • Mars Express, MAVEN, Tianwen-1: Orbital missions studying atmosphere, magnetosphere, and surface.
