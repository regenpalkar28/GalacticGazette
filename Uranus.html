Uranus

Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third-largest by diameter. It is known for its unique sideways rotation—its axis is tilted over 90 degrees, making it roll around the Sun like a barrel. Uranus is an ice giant, with an atmosphere rich in hydrogen, helium, and methane, giving it a pale blue-green color.

Uranus in natural color as seen by Voyager 2

Physical Characteristics

  • Diameter: About 50,724 km
  • Mass: 8.681 × 1025 kg (14.5 times Earth's mass)
  • Rotation: About 17 hours, with extreme axial tilt of 97.8°
  • Moons: 27 known moons, including Titania, Oberon, and Miranda
  • Rings: 13 faint rings made mostly of dark particles

Chemical Composition

  • Atmosphere: Hydrogen, helium, and methane (which gives the planet its color)
  • Interior: Icy materials such as water, ammonia, and methane, surrounding a small rocky core

Space Expeditions

  • Voyager 2 (1986): The only spacecraft to visit Uranus; revealed details of its atmosphere, moons, and rings
  • Proposed Missions: NASA and ESA are considering future missions like the Uranus Orbiter and Probe to study the planet in more detail
